The Contenders

Oklahoma City Thunder — The Favorites

The Thunder had a season for the ages. At 64–18 they boast the best record in the league and the youngest core to ever do it. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander is playing at an all-time level, Chet Holmgren anchors the defense, and head coach Mark Daigneault has built a system everyone fears. The only question is whether playoff experience catches up with them.

San Antonio Spurs — Wemby's Coming-Out Party

Victor Wembanyama's sophomore season silenced every doubter. At 62–20, the Spurs are legitimate Finals contenders and Wembanyama looks like the most unstoppable force in basketball when he is healthy. San Antonio would love a Finals rematch scenario against a traditional powerhouse.

Detroit Pistons — The Biggest Story in Basketball

Just two seasons removed from a league-worst record, the Pistons finished 60–22 and claimed the top seed in the East. Their combination of size, depth, and relentless defense has the entire league on notice. If they make the Finals, it will be one of the greatest turnarounds in NBA history.

Boston Celtics — Defending Champions

Never count out the Celtics. The defending champions finished 56–26 and have the championship experience this bracket demands. Jayson Tatum and Jaylen Brown have done this before. Home-court advantage through the ECF is gone, but that may only fuel them more.
